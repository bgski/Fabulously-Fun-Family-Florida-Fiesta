Area Guides

Where you'll be hanging out

Universal CityWalk

Universal's open-air dining, shopping, and nightlife strip connecting all three parks. No ticket needed. Restaurants (Bigfire, Toothsome, Cowfish, Vivo), bars and clubs (Pat O'Brien's dueling pianos, Red Coconut Club, Rising Star karaoke), food courts, movie theater, Hollywood Drive-In mini golf, and the Universal Studios Store. Live music most evenings. Self-parking free after 6 PM.

üåê universalorlando.com/citywalk ‚Üí

International Drive (I-Drive)

Orlando's 14-mile tourist corridor. ICON Park (Orlando Eye, SEA LIFE Aquarium, Madame Tussauds, 40+ restaurants), Pointe Orlando (WonderWorks, nightlife), Fun Spot America (rides, go-karts), and Topgolf. Dinner shows (Medieval Times, Mango's, Pirates) plus Howl at the Moon all sit on or near I-Drive. I-Ride Trolley ($2/ride) runs the full strip.

üåê internationaldriveorlando.com ‚Üí

Disney Springs

120-acre open-air shopping/dining/entertainment complex at Walt Disney World. No ticket, free parking, free entry. Four neighborhoods: Marketplace (World of Disney), The Landing (waterfront dining ‚Äî Boathouse, Morimoto, Raglan Road), Town Center (Zara, Uniqlo, Lululemon, Sephora ‚Äî the main shopping strip), West Side (Splitsville bowling, AMC, Cirque du Soleil). 150+ shops and restaurants. Lakeside, walkable, well-landscaped.

üåê disneysprings.com ‚Üí

Cocoa Beach

Closest real ocean beach to Kissimmee ‚Äî about 75 miles east on the Atlantic coast. Historic Cocoa Beach Pier (800 feet, restaurants, bars, shops), laid-back surf-town vibe, views of Port Canaveral cruise ships. Wide walkable beach with golden sand. Downtown strip along N. Atlantic Ave has surf shops, casual seafood, and ice cream. Not a fancy resort beach ‚Äî it's a real Florida beach town.
